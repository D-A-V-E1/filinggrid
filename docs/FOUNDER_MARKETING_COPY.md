### Origin Story Narrative

#### The problem

Every earnings season, the same workflow repeats. Open five or ten EDGAR tabs — one per peer. Scroll to MD&A. Lose alignment. Open risk factors in another window. Copy a revenue recognition footnote into Excel just to compare wording. Repeat for the next ticker.

The data is public. The structure is familiar. The workflow is still painfully manual — and the clock keeps running before you can point to the outlier.

I knew this problem from two directions. As a financial reporting leader for publicly listed manufacturers and growth companies, I spent more than a decade on the **preparer side** — a dozen-plus annual filing cycles of 10-K and 10-Q work, XBRL tagging, restatements, segment reporting, and the disclosure support behind billion-dollar financings. During each ~90-day reporting cycle I was overwhelmed: close-to-file deadlines, information landing faster than anyone could absorb, and XBRL tagging workload that never quite let up. I knew how filings are built, where sections break, and why consistency across periods and peers matters.

What frustrated me most was what came *after* filing. That is when structured peer delta review should feed your next cycle — policy changes to watch, risk language peers dropped, footnotes that diverge from the peer set. But you rarely have the bandwidth or the tooling to do it well. You are exhausted from the cycle you just closed, and the read side still runs on tabs, scroll drift, and Excel paste.

As the founder and operator of **M Cafe** in Taichung, Taiwan, I learned the same lesson in a different industry: **operators need clarity fast**. POS data, cost analysis, pricing decisions — friction kills good decisions.

#### The insight

The gap was not access to data. EDGAR is free. The gap was **time to the outlier** — getting the same disclosure section, the same fiscal period, the same financial context, across multiple issuers in one view, without rebuilding the layout every quarter.

Peer Disclosures does not solve the close or the tagging workflow. It addresses the read-and-compare layer — the post-filing review that should sharpen your next cycle but usually gets skipped or half-done because nothing is built for it.

I also knew from years of XBRL implementation that structured financial data and narrative disclosure belong together — not in separate tools, not buried in raw HTML.

The best reporting tools I had built or used shared one trait: they reduced manual reconciliation. Peer disclosure review — what I now call **delta diving**, digging into the differences and outliers between peer filings — deserved the same treatment.

#### The solution

**Peer Disclosures** streams 10-K, 10-Q, 20-F, and 6-K filings from EDGAR, parses them into comparable sections, and renders a synchronized multi-column workspace. Jump to Business, Risk Factors, MD&A, or footnotes once — every column follows. XBRL headline metrics load alongside the narrative; Professional adds full GAAP statements, eight columns, filing history, and saved peer groups.

Free for three tickers. No login required. Public data cached for speed. Research use only — verify against EDGAR, as any serious user should.

Built by a reporting veteran who filed the documents — and an entrepreneur who respects what it means to work under deadline. The comparison layer so you are not starting from scratch each quarter.
