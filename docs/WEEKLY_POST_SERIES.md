## Series framework

### Series name

**Delta Diving Diaries** — weekly first-person notes from people who compare peer SEC filings for real work, not product demos.

Subtitle option for pinned post: *One persona, one workflow, one outlier spotted.*

### Posting cadence

| Guideline | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Frequency** | Once per week — same weekday if possible (Tuesday or Wednesday works well; avoid Monday inbox noise) |
| **Length** | 150–250 words in the body; hook stands alone as line 1 |
| **Media** | Optional: one screenshot of aligned MD&A or footnote columns (blur nothing sensitive — all public EDGAR data). Text-only is fine. |
| **Earnings tie-in** | When a post’s sector reports that week, link the suggested compare URL in the first comment as well as the post body |
| **Founder posts** | David’s voice (Week 12 capstone only in this batch) — max 1 in 4 weeks if the series continues beyond 12 |

### How to rotate personas

1. **One persona per week** — never blend roles in a single post.
2. **Cycle by workflow, not seniority** — alternate read-side (research, corp dev, investor) and preparer-side (reporting manager, advisory, IR) so the feed does not feel repetitive.
3. **Match compare URL to the story** — if the post mentions semis, link `nvda-vs-amd-vs-intc`; if banks, `jpm-vs-gs-vs-ms`. Add new slugs via [COMPARE_SLUGS_SEO.md](./COMPARE_SLUGS_SEO.md) when a sector lacks a preset.
4. **Concrete over generic** — each post must include at least one of: a section navigated (MD&A, Note 2, Risk Factors), a delta spotted, or minutes saved vs. EDGAR tabs.
5. **Soft CTA only** — invite try/compare/feedback; no “sign up now” pressure. Free tier needs no login.
6. **Quarterly refresh** — after 12 weeks, swap tickers for current reporting season; keep persona structure.

**Suggested rotation order (this series):** Research → Preparer → Advisory → Corp dev → IR → Retail investor → CFO → Legal → FP&A → Student → Founder (startup) → Founder (David capstone).
